01 · Your Role

Champion, Not Just User

Being a champion means you're the product owner of AI on your team. Same accountability, same gate — just faster cycles.

🔑

Access Owner

You hold the credentials. You decide who on your team gets access, when, and for how long. Two sessions active at a time — treat it like a shared physical resource.

🛡️

Quality Gate

Like a PO reviewing a feature before it ships — you review AI-generated outputs before they enter decisions, documents, or delivery. Not a blocker. A filter.

⚡

Speed Enabler

Your job is to make your team move faster with AI — not add process. A clear gate speeds things up because people stop second-guessing what to use AI for.

📐

Standard Setter

You define what "good" looks like for AI usage on your team. What prompts work. What output needs review. What doesn't need AI at all.

02 · Access SOP

How Access Works

One account. Two active sessions max. You manage the rotation. This is physical access — treat it accordingly.

✅
You choose the two teammates. Based on current sprint priorities. You can rotate teammates each sprint if the work demands it.
🚫
No more than 2 simultaneous sessions. If both teammates are active and you need to use it too — one teammate wraps up first before you start.
🔄
Rotation is your call. If a teammate is hoarding the session with low-value tasks, you reassign. You decide what's worth the seat.
📝
Keep a simple log. Just a shared note: who used it, what for, outcome. Takes 30 seconds. Helps you identify high-value patterns over time.

Rotation SOP — Step by Step

1

Teammate requests access

Teams message or ping the champion: "Need Claude for [task type] — approx 30 mins." Quick, not formal.

2

Champion checks active sessions

Is anyone currently on it? If 2 are active, teammate waits. If a slot is free, champion gives the go-ahead and shares credentials.

3

Teammate uses it, logs the output

Completes the task. Saves/pastes meaningful outputs into a shared doc or sprint board — not lost in chat history.

4

Hand back + quick note

Teammate notifies champion: "Done — used it for [sprint notes / POC draft / research]. Output saved to [location]."

5

Champion reviews if output enters a decision

If the AI output is going into a client doc, sprint plan, or product decision — champion reads it first. Takes 2 minutes. See The Gate section.

03 · Usage Patterns

How to Actually Use It

AI is a thinking partner, not a search engine. These are the four patterns that give you real leverage in daily delivery and product work.

AI as Sprint Planner

Turn raw backlog chaos into structured sprint inputs in under 20 minutes.

claude.ai · Sprint Planning
You
Here are 12 items from our backlog [paste]. We have 2 weeks, 3 devs at 80% capacity. Help me identify the highest-priority 6 items and flag any blockers or dependencies between them.
Claude
Recommended sprint (6 items):
1. [Item A] — unblocked, 3 days
2. [Item B] — depends on A finishing first
3. [Item C] — independent, 2 days
...

Flagged: Items 7 and 9 have a shared dependency on the auth module — don't split them across sprints.
Always give Claude the capacity constraint upfront — it changes the output significantly.
Paste raw backlog items as-is. Claude can work with messy inputs.
Ask it to flag blockers separately — that's usually the most valuable output.
Champion reviews the sprint plan before it goes to the team. 5 minutes.

AI as POC Builder

Draft a proof of concept fast — to validate an idea before committing sprint capacity.

claude.ai · POC Draft
You
We want to add an automated grant eligibility check in mGrant. Before we spec it out, help me draft a rough POC — what inputs are needed, what the logic looks like, and what the output to the user should be. Just enough to validate the idea with the product team.
Claude
POC Draft — Grant Eligibility Check

Inputs needed: Org type, FCRA status, CSR budget year, application category...
Core logic: Rule-based first pass → flag edge cases for manual review...
Output: Eligible / Not Eligible / Needs Review with reason code...

This can be built as a Frappe DocType with a server-side validation hook. Estimated effort: 3–5 dev days.
POC output goes to champion first — champion decides if it's worth putting in a formal spec.
Frame it as "help me think through" not "build me" — that gets better structured output.
Always include the constraint: stack, team size, timeline. Claude adjusts its suggestions.

AI as Daily Thinking Partner

Unblock yourself on decisions, articulate problems clearly, and prep for calls — fast.

claude.ai · Daily Use
You
I'm about to have a call with a client who's frustrated about reporting delays. Help me structure my talking points: what to acknowledge, what to commit to, and what to push back on.
Claude
Acknowledge: The delay is real and caused friction. Own it early.

Commit to: A specific date for the next report, not a range. One concrete action by EOW.

Push back on: Any request to change the reporting format mid-cycle — that adds delay, not reduces it. Offer a review after this cycle closes instead.
Use it for standup prep — paste your tasks and ask "what's blocking what?" for a 2-minute clarity check.
Great for drafting difficult messages or emails before you send them.
Don't use it to avoid thinking — use it to think faster. Start with your own view, then test it.

AI as Prompt Reviewer

Champion reviews teammate outputs the way a PO reviews a ticket — before it enters any decision.

claude.ai · Review Mode
Champion (You)
My teammate used Claude to draft a client-facing status update. Here it is: [paste]. Review it — flag anything that's inaccurate, overpromises, or reveals internal process the client shouldn't see.
Claude
Flagged (3 items):
1. "By next week" — too vague, remove or specify a date.
2. "Our team is still finalizing internally" — remove. Clients don't need to know this.
3. Paragraph 3 is accurate and clear — no changes needed.

Suggested rewrite for para 1: ...
The review only applies to outputs that enter decisions, client docs, or sprint plans. Not every message needs a gate.
If you review it and it's fine — say so and move on. The gate should take 2 minutes, not 20.
Build a shared "rejected prompts" list over time — patterns of what Claude gets wrong for your context are more valuable than individual corrections.

05 · Standards

When to Use AI — Quick Table

Use this to decide in under 10 seconds. If it's not on the YES list, it doesn't need Claude.

Task Use AI? Notes
Sprint planning — prioritising backlog items Yes Give it capacity + context. Champion reviews plan output.
Drafting client status update Gate Use it to draft. Champion reviews before sending.
POC for a new feature idea Yes Best use. Gets ideas to "reviewable" stage fast.
Summarising a long thread or MOM Yes Paste and ask for bullets. Verify key decisions manually.
Structuring a call agenda or prep Yes Give it context + goal. Very fast win.
Generating numbers or financial estimates No Claude will hallucinate specifics. Use your real data.
Analysing or referencing client data No Never paste client PII, grant data, or financials.
Writing a product requirement or spec Gate Claude drafts, champion validates against actual context.
Answering a domain question you know the answer to No Don't outsource thinking you can do in 30 seconds.
06 · Limits

What Not To Do

Short list. Non-negotiable. These aren't guidelines — they're stops.

🚫

No client data

Never paste grant beneficiary data, financials, PII, or any client-specific identifiers into Claude. Even in summaries.

🚫

No credential sharing beyond your 2

You own it. Two people only. If a third person needs access — that goes to Ravi or Nihaan, not around the system.

🚫

Don't trust numbers blindly

Claude will generate plausible-sounding estimates and statistics. Always verify before they enter a document.

🚫

Don't skip the gate on client-facing output

If it's going to a client — it goes through the champion. No exceptions, even if you're under time pressure.

🚫

Don't use it as a search engine

Claude doesn't browse the web by default. Don't ask it for current events, live data, or "what's the latest on X."

🚫

Don't replace judgement

AI accelerates your thinking — it doesn't replace it. If an output feels wrong, trust your gut and question the output.

07 · Quick Reference

Copy-Paste Prompts

Start here. Adapt as you learn what works for your context.

Sprint Planning
Here are [N] backlog items [paste list]. We have [X] weeks, [Y] devs at [Z]% capacity. Prioritise the top [N] items, flag dependencies, and note anything that shouldn't be split across sprints.
POC Draft
I want to validate an idea before speccing it: [describe idea]. Help me draft a rough POC — what inputs are needed, what the core logic looks like, and what the user-facing output should be. Keep it at concept level, not implementation.
Client Update Review
Here's a draft client update [paste]. Flag anything that: (1) overpromises, (2) reveals internal process the client shouldn't see, (3) is factually vague or could be misread. Suggest fixes only for flagged items.
Call Prep
I have a call with [role/context] about [topic]. The situation is: [2-3 sentences]. Help me structure talking points: what to acknowledge, what to commit to, and what to push back on — with a suggested opening line.
MOM Summary
Here are notes from a meeting [paste]. Pull out: (1) decisions made, (2) action items with owners, (3) open questions not resolved. Format as bullets. Don't add anything that wasn't in the notes.
Blocker Articulation
I'm stuck on [describe blocker]. Help me articulate it clearly: what exactly is blocked, what I've already tried, and what I need from whom to unblock it. I'll use this to communicate it to [stakeholder].
