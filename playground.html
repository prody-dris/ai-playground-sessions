Architecture

A2A Architecture

Ankit Jangir · Nihaan Mohammed

How do agents talk to each other without becoming a tangle? We're mapping the protocol layer underneath multi-agent systems — handoffs, state, context boundaries.

What we're solving
  • The contract for an agent-to-agent call — request, context, return
  • Where one agent owns context vs. delegates it cleanly
  • What a registry of agents looks like — and what shouldn't go in it
Where you'd plug in
  • You've felt agents stepping on each other in your own work
  • You want a say in what the protocol enforces vs. leaves loose
Product

HRMS Ticketing MVP

Akshat Verma · Kashish Talwani

Internal HR queries are scattered across DMs and email. We're building the first working slice of an in-house ticketing flow on HRMS — minimal, real, shippable this sprint.

What we're solving
  • The thinnest ticket lifecycle that's still useful — raise, triage, resolve
  • Where it lives in HRMS without bloating the form
  • What HR actually needs to see vs. what's nice-to-have
Where you'd plug in
  • You've raised an HR ticket recently and have opinions about what hurt
  • You've shipped MVPs before and know how to keep one small

The hour

How the room runs.

1
Bring it

Drop your problem in the meeting or in the WhatsApp group. Forward-looking, half-broken, fully stuck — all welcome.

2
Frame it

Five minutes to walk us through what's stuck and what good looks like. The room asks the questions you forgot to ask yourself.

3
Build it

Pair off. Attack it for the rest of the hour. Tools out, screens shared, real progress — not theory.

4
Leave with it

Someone owns the next step. A short recap drops by Tuesday on this page. Next Thursday, fresh problems.
