Session 6 · The Collective Craft
Of Chaos and Craft.
Thursday, 23 April 2026 · 5:00–6:00 PM · 5MEVNground

Chaos when ten people build their own way. Craft when a team builds one way. Thursday, we learn how to get there.

Why this session

Five sessions got each of us independently good at AI. Individual craft is solved. But individual craft does not compose — ten people building ten different ways is chaos, not output. S6 is where individual craft becomes collective craft.

33%
Unguided Claude attempts that succeed — Anthropic, internal
41%
Higher revert rate for AI code vs human code — GitClear 2024, 150M LoC

The fix isn’t more prompting practice. It’s shared standards. Plugins, skills, conventions, guardrails. Not documentation to read — infrastructure the team runs on. 2026 has converged on this: Martin Fowler, Stack Overflow, Sourcegraph, JetBrains, CodeRabbit all landed on the same sentence — treat AI instructions as infrastructure: versioned, reviewed, shared artifacts that encode tacit team knowledge into executable instructions.

Four layers a team needs. Every layer we have already taught — Thursday we connect them.

What we’ll cover

1

Why team AI needs structure. Why plugins matter. Why standards matter. Why uniformity matters. The opening — short, blunt, grounded in 2026 evidence. You can’t scale individual AI by adding more people; you scale it by encoding the standard.
