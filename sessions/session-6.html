Session 6 · The Collective Craft
Of Chaos and Craft.
Thursday, 23 April 2026 · 5:00–6:00 PM · 5MEVNground

Chaos when ten people build their own way. Craft when a team builds one way. Thursday — how we get there.

Why this session

Five sessions made each of us good at AI on our own. But good-on-your-own does not add up to a good team. Ten people building ten different ways is chaos — not output. Session 6 is the bridge. Individual craft → collective craft.

33%
Unguided Claude attempts that succeed — Anthropic, internal
41%
Higher revert rate on AI code vs human code — GitClear, 150M LoC

The fix is not more prompting practice. It is shared standards — plugins, skills, conventions, guardrails. Not documents to read. Infrastructure the team runs on.

In 2026, the whole industry has said the same thing. Treat your AI instructions the way you treat your code. Version them. Review them. Share them.

The Stack of Shared Craft

Four layers a team needs. We have taught each layer already. Thursday, we connect them.

1

One set of rules everyone follows. The base instructions.

S5 → S6

Shared Guardrails

Hooks, review gates, AI-SDLC. What catches bad output before it leaves your machine.

S4 + now

The first question to carry in

Before you build anything — ask
Did someone already solve this?

Plugins and skills are shared craft. Someone on the team, someone in the community, someone at Anthropic may have already done the work. Your first move is not to build — it is to look.

Ten people each building the same thing three different ways → chaos.  One person building it well, nine using it → craft.

What we’ll cover

1

Why team AI needs structure. Why plugins. Why standards. Why uniformity. Short and blunt. You don’t scale individual AI by adding more people — you scale it by sharing the standard.

2

Reuse first. The one question to ask before you build anything — “did someone already solve this?” The psychology that turns chaos into craft.

3

AI-SDLC & the Plugin Guardians. Ankit. Team AI is a system, not a pile of individual setups. Plugins, the guardians who maintain them, PR discipline, review gates — the full lifecycle. A related session covered this ground earlier this week; Thursday a short carry-over.

4

Anthropic & the learning path. Dhwani begins Anthropic Academy. Structured learning, straight from the team that builds Claude. Open to everyone.

5

The Collective Craft in practice. Aditya Gore — how he organises AI through his development pipeline, how he enforces discipline, the clean setup that makes team output add up. Abhinav — design as psychology, science, structure. Not “what looks good.”
